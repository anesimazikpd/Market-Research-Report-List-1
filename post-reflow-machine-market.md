<p><p>The post-reflow machine market encompasses various technologies essential for PCB assembly and soldering processes. Convection ovens utilize heated air circulation to evenly distribute heat during soldering, ensuring uniform melting of solder. Conversely, vapor phase ovens employ a non-toxic fluid that vaporizes at a controlled temperature, allowing for precise heating of components without thermal shock. Both types serve critical roles in enhancing solder joint reliability and are increasingly sought after in the electronics manufacturing industry for their efficiency and effectiveness.</p></p>
