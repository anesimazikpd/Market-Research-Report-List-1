<p><p>The blood thinner market is primarily divided into two types: injectable and oral blood thinners. Injectable blood thinners, such as low molecular weight heparins and direct thrombin inhibitors, are typically administered in healthcare settings or at home for immediate anticoagulation. Oral blood thinners, like warfarin and novel anticoagulants, are taken as pills and offer convenience for long-term management of conditions like atrial fibrillation and venous thromboembolism. Both types play crucial roles in preventing blood clots and managing cardiovascular risks.</p></p>
