<p><p>The external trauma fixators market comprises several types used for stabilizing fractures. Unilateral fixators involve a single frame applied to one side of the limb, while bilateral fixators use frames on both sides for enhanced stability. Circular fixators provide a comprehensive 360-degree support, suited for complex fractures. Hybrid fixators combine elements of both unilateral and circular designs, allowing for customizable stabilization. Additionally, "others" encompass unique or specialized fixators tailored for specific orthopedic needs, expanding treatment flexibility.</p></p>
